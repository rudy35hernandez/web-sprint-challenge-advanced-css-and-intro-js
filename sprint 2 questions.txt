1. How would you describe acessibility on the web to someone new to programming?

 	When speaking about accessibility, you have to factor in every person and device imaginable. For people with sight problems, you 
	have to make sure to add descriptions to the page if there are picture or items that do not have letter to be read out. As far as
	devices go, you want to make your webpages compatible with all device. Each device may have different screen size and pixel ratio. 
	Because of that, your webpage has to adjust to whatever size the window ends up being on any given device.

2. Talk about 3 different things you can do to ensure your website is accessible.

	Adding descriptions to items that have no words is a big help to blind people. Adding code to change the resolution to a page
	when a window in smaller or bigger than the original size. And lastly, the syntax used to built your HTML is vital. Things like
	h1, title, main, footer are all indicators of importance to the user, and also to the search engines.

3. How would you explain the concept of a variable to someone new to programming?
	A variable is a container of information/data. You can call upon that variable anytime you want in your code.
